EDUCATION IN KENYA

The national educational system consists of three levels: eight years of compulsory primary education (beginning at age six), four years at the secondary level, and four years of higher education. The government provides free primary and secondary education.

Education for the indigenous population was not a priority of the British colonial government. After independence, however, primary and secondary school enrollment expanded markedly. One consequence of this educational expansion was that underemployment and unemployment increased as better-educated citizens entered the job market.
